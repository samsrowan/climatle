    What is Climatle?

    Climatle is a daily guessing game designed to help people learn about climate change mitigation across countries. Each day, you're shown three visualizations of a mystery country's climate profile — its greenhouse gas emissions by sector, its electricity generation mix, and its emissions trajectory over time — and you have six attempts to guess which country it is.

    After each guess, you receive feedback on how similar your guess is to the target: cosine similarity scores for emissions and energy profiles, geographic distance and direction, and comparisons of GHG and GDP per capita. Use the clues to narrow down your next guess!

    Data sources

    • Sectoral GHG emissions (2021): Minx et al., 2021 (Earth System Science Data), GWP100 AR6 weighting. Covers CO2, CH4, N2O, and fluorinated gases across 25 subsectors.
    • Electricity generation (2024): Ember Global Electricity Review. Shares of generation by fuel source.
    • Emissions trajectories (1990–2024): Annual national GHG totals from Minx et al., 2021 — the same dataset as the sectoral breakdown above, extended through 2024.
    • NDC targets: Nationally Determined Contributions compiled from the UNFCCC NDC Registry. These are countries' climate mitigation pledges under the Paris Agreement. Targets shown are current to COP26 (2021).
